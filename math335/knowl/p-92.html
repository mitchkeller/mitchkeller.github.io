If \(a\) and \(b\) are the two numbers, let \(b>a\) without loss of generality. The key is that \(b-a>0\text{,}\) so by the corollary to the Archimedean Axiom we have that \(b-a>1/N>0\) for some natural number \(N\text{.}\) So \(Nb-Na>1\text{,}\) which means \(Nb>m>Na\) for some integer \(m\) — you can show this in several ways, anyway with Archimedean one way or another — and so \(b>m/N>a\text{.}\)
