Take the point between \(x\) and \(y\) that Point Set Axiom 2 guarantees, and form an open interval between it and something else, containing \(x\) and not \(y\text{.}\) Then after some \(N\) all terms of the sequence are in this interval. Now find an interval starting at that point and containing \(y\) and not \(x\text{.}\) There is definitely no \(M\) with everything after that in it, since there is a point past which nothing is in it!
