Preface Introduction

This course guide will serve as the only reference to the concepts generally allowed for the course. As we encounter them, all relevant definitions and problems will appear, along with any guides to exploration. I will update the version of the notes posted online as the semester progresses. I do this so that I add additional helpful items based on the pacing of the course without making a mess of something you might have already printed.

A Definition is just that. However, in this type of course, it is extremely important to use definitions very accurately, and we try to use as few as possible to avoid confusion and oversaturation. They are numbered to make them easy to find, but definitions are generally referred to by naming the term being defined rather than by number.

A Theorem, Lemma, or Corollary is something to be proved. Some (such as corollaries) will be very easy, a few extremely hard. Most will be somewhere in between, to enhance your senses of confidence and accomplishment. Most class time will be hearing and responding to your proofs of these items (or working in groups on proving them); they are the core of the course. Any theorem is eligible to be submitted to the class journal for publication. Generally, if a lemma supports a theorem or a corollary follows a theorem, those would be bundled together with the theorem as a single publication. If different students present proofs of these results, they could submit a joint paper if they so desired.

An Exercise or Problem is generally not an item available for the class journal, but it should usually be very helpful in understanding the content (and hopefully proofs) of the theorems. These items will generally be instructions for trying to create your own examples or open-ended investigation meant to lead you toward making a conjecture or better understanding definitions. Occasionally a problem will ask you to “prove or disprove” or “prove or disprove and salvage”. Some of these, if stated as a theorem or counterexample and theorem, may qualify for the class journal. Please ask if you have one of these that you would like to submit for publication. I will let you know if I think it qualifies.
