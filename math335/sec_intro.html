Section 1 Introduction

This course guide will serve as the only reference to the concepts generally allowed for the course. As we encounter them, all relevant definitions and problems will appear, along with any guides to exploration. I will print out pages for you as they appear, but please feel free to make lots of notes!

A Problem is something to be proved. Some will be very easy, a few extremely hard. Most will be somewhere in between, to enhance your senses of confidence and accomplishment. Most class time will be hearing and responding to your solutions to these items; they are the core of the course.

A Definition is just that. However, in this type of course, it is extremely important to use definitions very accurately, and we try to use as few as possible to avoid confusion and oversaturation. Hence, they are highlighted just as much as problems.

An Example is not a problem available for the portfolio, but it should usually be very helpful in understanding the content (and hopefully proofs) of the problems. It may be a worked-out example I provide (perhaps in class), or it may be some instructions for trying your own examples.
